The Process

Four weeks. One case. Zero mercy.

Every month, a new case file lands in your account. Over four weeks, evidence unfolds and the truth gets harder to ignore.

Week 01

The Crime Scene

Your case file drops. Victim profile, crime scene analysis, initial witness statements, and your first look at the suspect pool. Start building your theory.

Week 02

The Evidence

Forensic results arrive. Alibis are tested. Timelines crack. New witness audio, lab reports, and surveillance details surface contradictions.

Week 03

The Twist

Everything shifts. New evidence emerges. A suspect's story changes. Connections between characters surface that nobody saw coming.

Week 04

The Solve

Submit your verdict: who did it, how, and why. The official case debrief drops. See how your theory stacks up and climb the ranks.

"The Hargrove Inheritance"

On the morning of February 14th, Margaret Hargrove, 78, was found unresponsive in the sunroom of her 12-acre estate in Ashford, North Carolina. Cause of death: cardiac arrest, complicated by an unidentified compound found in her evening tea. Her will, amended 72 hours before her death, redirected $4.2 million away from her three adult children and toward an unknown trust. All three children were present at the estate that weekend. None of them have a clean alibi.
